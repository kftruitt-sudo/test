In November of 1990, the United States Senate passed H.R.5237—the Native American Graves Protection and Repatriation Act (NAGPRA). This law required institutions that received federal funding to document, protect, and return sacred artifacts and ancestors to their source communities. When it comes to possessing sacred indigenous artifacts, UCSB is one of the worst offenders. As noted by Debra Herrick, Associate Editorial Director of The Current, about “700 Native ancestors and approximately 22,000 funerary objects” have entered UCSB’s campus. These are staggering numbers, made worse by the glacial process of documenting and repatriating. NAGPRA has been around since 1990, and UCSB, after 36 years, is still not compliant.
